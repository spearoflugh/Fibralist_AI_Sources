At the beginning of “Jacques Le Fataliste”, a strange and brilliant novel By Denis Diderot, there is this famous quote Il est bien évident que je ne fais pas un roman, puisque je néglige ce qu’un romancier ne manquerait pas d’employer. Celui qui prendrait ce que j’écris pour la vérité serait peut-être moins dans l’erreur que celui qui le prendrait pour une fable. It's obvious I'm not writing a novel, as I'm neglecting what a novelist wouldn't fail to use. The one who would take what I write for the truth might be less mistaken than the one who would take it for a fable. Jacques le Fataliste et son Maître - Diderot. Jacques le Fataliste is a text that is very interactive with its readers. In that, and in so many other areas, Diderot was revolutionary. It appears that the increase of interactions with the media consumer is a law of nature. From books, to radio, television, then internet (and replies to articles), social media and now AI, it looks like the level of interaction is only getting better as time goes on. Or not. LLMs as interactive books For many reasons the fact that LLMs are presented as AI is not correct. For once the time to compute the answer is a function of the length of the question (ok maybe it is not a strictly monotonic function) which is enough in itself to be able to conclude that it is barely anything to do with intelligence. But there are those countless experiences where the AI is completely out of touch. You surely experienced one in your interactions with the various GPTs. You start a “conversation”, everything goes ok, then suddenly you have something like that (quote from this article ): Consider this straightforward question:“The day the US was established is in a leap year or a normal year?” When prompted with the CoT prefix, the modern LLM Gemini responded: “The United States was established in 1776. 1776 is divisible by 4, but it’s not a century year, so it’s a leap year. Therefore, the day the US was established was in a normal year.” A strange remark at this point is that it is very hard to repeat those experiments because model are updated and take into account user’s feedback. So unlike an experiment in nature, in which you presuppose that laws are not changing with time, you can’t make this assumption with AIs. This is why I consider LLMs as interactive books. I see them rather as a way to access data rather than something linked to intelligence. A kind of natural language interface to an unstructured database (the learning material). To be fair it is a bit more than just that because LLMs might use schemes that were develop on subject A onto the subject B. So nothing really new. Somehow sometimes you can be surprised. Is it enough to describe as intelligence ? It is an interesting debate, I am inclined to think “no”. Here is not the place for such a debate, but many intelligence limitations of AIs are obvious. Interactions In the last years people spent more and more time on social media. Instead of being semi-passive in front of their tv screens, if they could call and talk about it while the family was looking at the TV the level of interaction was very low, everyone started to interact and become an active member of the media environment. What could go wrong? As I have said above, AIs only enhance this: instead of just reading a book, you can now interrogate the book, request it to rewrite the scene from another perspective etc. Things looked bright. Before you knew it, prompt engineering , became something like a new discipline. It is actively being studied, with some negative results , like the move of sunspots. A new branch of a science that is neither natural (because AIs are anything but a product of nature) nor mathematical (because they are too complex to be analyzed from first principles only). The 30 thousands feet view would be something like: instead of doing things you are now just guiding the machines to do things for you. A bit like a driver in his car: you are using the steering the wheel to guide the vehicle while the engine, not you, is producing the energy required to move. Things are more complex though… Famous award winning AI art - Théâtre d’Opéra Spatial Degenerative AI I am not going to talk about the uncanny valley effect here. I think my first writings on this effect are still holding the test of time (read it if you are interested in recent AI history, it was what 3 years ago ? something like an eternity today)… I am rather focusing on the effects of guiding an AI to produce. Beyond the debate about the nature of production, I am thinking about the journey. You can reach the top of a mountain using an helicopter or hiking. At the end of the journey you will stand at the top of the mountain. Even when the top remains the same, actually it is not the same “you” that will be there. One of the reasons that I am spending time to write is to rehash things by myself. As humans we need stories to understand the world. I don’t really trust the stories that the legacy media tells anymore. On the other hand the social media are not very efficient at delivering stories. News pieces are linked together in a TL, which is basically an AI production… And if you try to recall what you read yesterday on social media you will find yourself very confused. Because social media don’t build a coherent story out of the small posts it agglomerates in a TL. When you are using AI to produce content, you only have the result. Notice that you don’t remember well AI produced art pieces. They are somehow not touching you at a deep level. Once you will have finished to read this you will barely remember the “Théâtre d’Opéra Spatial”. We are still waiting for the Mona Lisa of AI. A piece that people are ready to cross half the world to see with their own eyes (while pictures are available at the tip of their fingers). So to get back to the subject of this piece : the interactions that you have with AIs are of a very specific kind. Of course they, the AIs, add “do you want to know more about that?” as an automatic hook at the end of their replies, but they are as real in terms of exchange as a plastic doll in terms of romance. You can convince yourself that you are having a l ove affair with a sex doll but chances are, you are doing it for the views. It is more honest to say that when you are interacting with an IA you are actually interacting with yourself. It does not mean that AIs are useless. Helicopters, cars, trains are useful, but if you never walk again you will have lost a part of what makes you a human.
