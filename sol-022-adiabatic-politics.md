Vibe coding is rehashing old programs using prompts and AI as intermediary tools. It should be called adiabatic programming instead, borrowing the term from thermodynamics where adiabatic processes are phenomenon occuring without heat transfer. On a broader social scale the same thing is happening with politics. In both cases the results are far from perfection and demands close attention in order to sort the wheat from the chaff. With thermodynamic lenses such processes, ideally, are isentropic: they are reversible which means nothing really new never appears (the process is somehow reversible). Fukuyama's ends and means The end of history, seen as the never-ending win of liberal democracy as the only political system has more merits than generally attributed. It is easy to put a map on the table and check where the fault lines and conflicts are, real or in the waiting room, and conclude that Huntington's predictions were more on the point. Both can be true simultaneously. I would split the battle there: reality gives the point to Huntington and ideology to Fukuyama. If you take a step aside and look at the political landscape it isn't easy to spot that there are almost no serious political propositions. We are left in a perpetual crisis but most commentators are stuck like flies inside a glass jar. 2025 utopias are either completely stupid or naive rehash of some XIX philosophy. Some, like James Lindsay, are even making a living about that. Others, like Curtis Yarvin, are identifying the fundamental issues (like the structural preference for positives) but do not convince on their proposal of actions. The return of the King (the CEO)? Really? Is it a fit system for the digital age? I am not convinced for structural reasons that I am going to talk about later. Aside from the intellectual landscape, the activist theater is also becoming more and more incomprehensible. Take the cruise towards Gaza that appears like an insta story with Greta Thunberg and Rima Hasan (french representative in European parliament): they are using a sailboat, surely to save the earth from CO2, but with the sails down. They just altered the course of their cruise to gather a migrant boat while they were crossing Libya... At the end of the day it looks like a social media circus --there was the drone incident : a true caricature of the odyssey with selfies--. It shows no end, nor ends other than clutch, in sight. Don't hold your breath if you are waiting for an analysis or even the description of an utopia that the activists would strive for. It doesn't go farther than : "stop right now because I don't like that". Try to picture yourself the professional accomplishment of influencers on the course of few years. Digital acid A major problem of digital culture is its inherent lack of stability. This the source of many of the inanities outlined in the previous paragraph. With the ability to change things as fast as snapping fingers over a smartphone, most of us have forgotten that there is a real world that has inertia. Likewise seeing new apps everyday may make you forget that cultures and societies are necessarily built over the course of several generations. So you have this political (and technical) class that lures itself into thinking than an Instagram story is as impactful as the slow building of common laws and the establishment of traditions. The lack of stability at the highest levels of institutions has been recently highlighted by the end of Doge and the very public divorce between Musk and Trump. Musk spent most of his time (and if time is money given his level it represents a non negligible amount of money), energy and personal funds to help Trump in his race to the White House. (Note the Musk/Trump just got nuclear while writing this post more on that later) Intersectionality What happens inside a mental jar after a sufficiently long time ? Every idea is just the rehash of the rehash of the rehash of old things. Nothing new emerges. But humans are wired to have a strong preference for novelty. It is maybe most visible in men sexual preferences: the most beautiful girl of the world is the next one. On the political arena the issue is to make something new with old recipes. The answer as usual comes from social media in form of the race to stupidity in order to gather views: from the Costco family to the guy using dice to make sandwiches mixing ingredients at random. The same mechanism is at play across the political landscape. Just take a look at the Democrats berating Trump for either not spending (DOGE) or spending to much (the infamous big beautiful bill) etc. At the end of the day the mental landscape looks like an isolated area where nothing really new never occurs. Have you noticed that whatever the news it is 1939 again and again? If institutions are still locked in a 1946 mindset, civil society is moving away faster and faster. People move around constantly. They are called migrants now: not immigrants or emigrants but just “migrants” which shows the point that has been reached. Deep Tech is moving away from deep state and as in the story of the Babel tower both parts don’t speak the same language anymore. Beware the fall of the tower.
