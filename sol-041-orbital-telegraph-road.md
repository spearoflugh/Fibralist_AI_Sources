<content>
SpaceX and xAI have just announced their merger into a single entity. True to form, the move comes with an "Elonesque" plan to deploy millions of satellites dedicated to AI processing. Naturally, significant hurdles remain—most notably the self-evident challenges of thermal management in a vacuum and orbital congestion—and Musk is famous for his hyperbolic business promises.

Yet, Musk is also known for delivering earth-shattering technologies. Starlink is a reality, already operating thousands of satellites. Furthermore, SpaceX is currently launching more mass into orbit than any other player in human history. To put it in perspective: In 2025 alone, SpaceX delivered approximately 2,413 metric tons of payload to orbit—exceeding China's cumulative total of roughly 1,812 metric tons across its entire space program history (from 1970 through the end of 2025). Read that last sentence again, slowly, to let the scale sink in. Those are no longer just hyperbolic promises but facts.

We are witnessing a shift in the space industry that will fundamentally alter life on Earth. These changes aren't incremental; they are a "French Revolution 4.0" on AI steroids.

Topological Equality

The first step of the digital revolution was transforming speech—which once possessed the qualities of an object—into something that possesses all the qualities of an idea. Since Plato, we have known that objects and ideas do not share the same properties. One concrete implication of this shift is the loss of locality of speech. Before the digital age, information moved at the speed of objects; orders and armies marched at the same pace. The digital revolution shattered that harmony. Suddenly, speech acquired the properties of an idea. A tweet can be read instantaneously by anyone, anywhere. Yet, despite our progress in transport, humans must still move using relatively slow machines. A fundamental disharmony was introduced: words and swords no longer move at the same pace.

The space industry is now promising to resolve this disharmony. It is a banal but profound observation: every point on Earth is at an equal distance from orbit. For a satellite in polar orbit, every inch of the planet eventually passes beneath it. Space technology is a revolution that promises to make every point on Earth topologically equal. The space revolution is completing the digital revolution, but on the objective side of reality.

Telegraph Road

Do you remember what happened last time? The Dire Straits Telegraph Road lyrics tell the story: a man sets down his load, builds a cabin, others follow, then churches, schools, lawyers, rules, trains, mines, ore, hard times, war. Telegraph Road actually exists — US Highway 24 in Michigan — built to maintain telegraph lines. "Telegraph lines were first installed from the Detroit area south to the Monroe area in the mid-19th century... As these communication lines were installed, roadways were added as needed to provide access for maintenance." Words and swords.

The new frontier is space. And the first arrived will build the cabin and the winter store. In other words: they will set the rules. What is 100% certain is that space is going to be used to rule the earth from above. It is happening today with almost nothing in space — you could count on one hand the global services. Just like the number of internet servers in 1992. Today there are around 1.8 billion internet servers — almost 1 server for every 4 living humans. Talk about scaling.

Builders and Rulers

The merger between SpaceX and xAI occurred at the exact moment French police were searching X's Paris headquarters, as prosecutors issued a summons for the company's owner. In 2025, the European Union issued more than €4.5 billion in fines under the Digital Markets Act and Digital Services Act. The total revenue from Digital Services Taxes across Europe amounted to €3.5 billion. The EU currently generates more revenue from fining extra-European companies than from taxing them. All of Europe combined put 2.1 metric tons into orbit last year — a thousand times less than SpaceX alone.

The New Leviathan

The "Telegraph Road" of the 21st century is being paved with silicon and stardust, not asphalt. The merger of SpaceX and xAI is the definitive signal that the Words vs. Swords disharmony is being resolved. By placing the brain (AI) and the nervous system (Starlink) into the same orbital body, a topological high ground is being created where geography is irrelevant and traditional regulation is friction of the past. We are entering an era where sovereignty is no longer defined by the lines on a map, but by the altitude of your servers. The future belongs to those who build the road, not those who wait at the toll booth.
