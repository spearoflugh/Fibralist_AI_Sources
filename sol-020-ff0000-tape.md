The invisible part of human evolution is how much it has been driven by taboos and their overcoming, or not. The story is told around remarkable dates of things that actually happened: battles, empire founding, technological inventions, etc. The Greeks discovered the steam engine, the Hero’s aeolipile , but they never tried to improve on it for practical applications. They were not really interested in the applications in the first place. It was used for fun a bit like Sora today. One could think that in our digital era it were no longer the case. All the discussion around the singularity is about that right? AI is improving faster and faster, and the sky is the limit. At least this is the cover story. In reality there is a case to be made that the red tape wall is getting larger by the day thanks to the digital revolution. California Dreamin’ The California high speed train project started in the mid-1990s. In 1996 to be precise the California High-Speed Rail Authority (CHSRA) was formally established by the State Legislature to begin planning. Long, and costly, story short of this project is not possible. Three decades later the budget moved from 33 billions to 128 billions (estimated so the final bill will be larger) and yet no train has ever moved. Up to 2023, already 23 billions were spent. Here is a citation of where sits the project in september 2025 from Billions spent, miles to go article: The authority is quick to note that 463 miles of the 494-mile system has cleared the environmental review process and is “construction ready.” It also boasts of having laid 70 miles of guideway — meaning track, elevated structures, or other riding surface — and erected 57 structures. Yes you have read it right, the red tape is not yet totally cut on this multi decade project. There remain 31 mile to clear for the environmental review process… I am making fun of California but you can find examples everywhere the eyesight is gazing. For instance today in France/Germany, 30 trains have been bought that can’t be used across the border because… new legislation on emergency brakes that is not the same on both sides of the border. Too bad because those trains, 400 millions euros, were specifically dedicated to France-Germany travels… Wait, wasn’t the digital revolution supposed to render the administrative work more efficient? That’s only the seller point that you are seeing here. The backstage is a bit different. If computers are very nice objects and do as they are told, they are also very obtuse and do only what they are supposed to do. An unintended, and often overlooked, consequence of the digitization of the world is that Polanyi’s paradox is becoming a major force to reckon with. The formulation of this paradox is we can know more than we can tell . There are many ways to understand this paradox, but one is that most of your knowledge is not actually linked to words. You do more things than you understand either. For instance no one has a scientific explanation of what life is. Yet it is very easy to have children. Even ants or amebae can reproduce… But a computer is a stupid machine that can only do what it is told. This is especially true for red tape. You put all the constraints in a computer, and it will behave as a proof checker. I have seen this transformation at my level in the academic life: there is a unification of systems for the marks and grades. There is a single system that is supposed to do everything and manage the information of students from the enrollment to the graduation. And the system is the hell on earth for edge cases that weren’t anticipated. What used to rely on a human informal eventual consistency has now become constrained by the strict consistency requirements of the system. Digital ID and all that Keir Starmer, the UK prime minister, is still pushing hard to implement his —but is it his or has it been suggested to him is a legit question— digital ID requirement in order to work in the UK before the end of his mandate. This is the same story, the major selling point moved to “control of the immigration” to “cut the faff” for people trying to prove their identity in a range of scenarios, as well as showing they have to the right to work. So the promise is again that digital technologies are going to be used to cut the red tape and make your life easier. If the situation reminds you the one of California bullet train project in the 1996 it is not just a coincidence. A major trend of the digitization of the world is the multiplication of red tapes. More and more credentials are being required to do anything and everything in particular. For any service or good you have to fill in “profiles” and display the right QR-code. The fact that the same object, a smartphone, is used to display and check the validity of the authorization has this pernicious effect that every citizen is becoming an enforcer and a prisoner at the same time. Just like in the Pite ș ti experiment . So ok your life is going to be made easier, but from what ? Essentially from the problems that you self inflict on yourself. Such attempts at fine control of the population were never imagined. Partly because they were technologically impossible to implement. But it is just one part of the story. Actually machines, technology, come with their own requirements. You don’t have to go full Kacynski to notice that. Now the fact that thanks to smartphone the digitization of the world follows you in every instant of your life, it even goes with you to the bathroom, is something new. The traditional borders of personal life are dissolving at the same rate as the south border of the USA during the Biden’s administration. Expect to be forced to use red toilet paper before you had a chance to have a say in the matter.
