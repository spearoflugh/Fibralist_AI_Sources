      The Ship of Theseus paradox (replacing parts one by one while maintaining identity)
      assumes physical inertia: change is gradual, costly, sequential. The digital world
      eliminates this constraint entirely. When a new app version is distributed, the

      political systems: the Trump second administration is not a Ship-of-Theseus slow
      replacement but an instant app update. "The ship might as well have been turned
      into a spaceship." This makes identity continuity — of institutions, of nations,
      of alliances — a qualitatively new problem in the digital era.

It always sounds pompous to cite ancient Greek philosophical paradoxes. Yet if they remain discussed for millennia it is for a good reason: they point to our difficulties to understand the world. The philosophical story of the ship of Theseus is one of these discussions. The idea is around the notion of identity: if you repair a ship, and eventually end up by replacing every piece of the original ship, is it still the same ship? There are many ways to consider this paradox, but the underlying theme is of course the one of continuity of things. Even you, as a biological entity, change at every second. Cells die, others appear and nothing remains the same within *your* body. So what is this thing that doesn't change? By definition identity is what doesn't change, but you would be hard pressed to define what *it* is.

What is true for individuals is even more true for societies and civilisations. The question of stability is paramount. Since the end of World War II we are living under the so called liberal international order. I have defended the thesis that this order couldn't persist any longer because of the new information technologies. The fact that the speed of information and the speed of armies have been decorrelated was going to inevitably lead to huge change. We are in the midst of it. The first month and a half — only! — of the second Trump administration is reminding us that on a daily basis. We are no longer in the Ship of Theseus story where small parts are replaced one by one. The rhythm is so pronounced that the ship might as well have been turned into a spaceship!

## Disturbance in the Continuity

The default of human civilization was to fight for continuity. Inertia is a powerful force and the case can be made that the whole enterprise of technology is to defeat it. Just to maintain society as it was required dedication, but to make it change was too much. We needed many tools for that. Material tools (industrial revolution) and mental tools (the idea of progress itself). Things accelerated until we reached the release speed when everyone got access to internet everywhere at any time. Trump I appeared as an anomaly for many: yes he was elected by social media but we — the permastate — are going to deal with it. The Biden intermezzo was looking like a return to normalcy while it was the anomaly. The waking up is brutal for many that were fed by mainstream media. Governing by buzz words does not work anymore. Bukele then Milei confirmed that the narrative was shifting. A new style of politicians is emerging. The thing is that, as usual, the present is not evenly distributed across the globe. Most European leaders are still living in the XIX. This is why this situation may appear so strange: there are changes that are impossible to miss, yet at the same time not every place has evolved at the same pace. This is why there is this feeling of disturbance. After all if everything changed, like it happened just after WWII, all across the globe then people would be less disoriented. Remind how Japan and Germany who had suffered large destructions were rising up again as economic powers in less than a generation. Today we have a strange mix of countries post social media and others pre social media. On top of that there are competing agendas for what is coming next, but those agendas are not really linked to traditional political oppositions. This is another line of fault adding to the confusion.

The dimensions on which the battles are being fought today are the Local/Global, Soft/Kinetic, Reform/Revolution axis. The Trump administration is radically set on Local/Soft/Revolution which is in total opposition to the EU that is still adhering to the Global/Kinetic/Reform sides of the equation. Biden administration was in harmony with EU on those dimensions. This is partly why the change is so brutal. Also the unusual combination Soft/Revolution is something weird of its own. Trump withdraws from the wars, he wants the Ukraine and Gaza things to be over as soon as possible. This is for the Soft dimension. For the Revolution dimension, the first weeks of the administration were clear: the press accreditations, the DOGE, the nomination of Gabbard, Patel and RFK are all promises of revolution — for the moment within the frame of the constitution — not reform. The shape that it takes is very post social media too: the activity of VP Vance on X is also clear. Notice how Vance writes his post himself (not a PR staffer) and how he engages personally. Compare with the use of social media by European leaders: they are using X but in a pre social media way: messages are written by PR teams, and are more like "TV by other means" than real social media interactions. So everything has changed, form and substance, overnight. This dramatic change reminds us that we are living in a digital era. When a new version of an app is distributed, the product can change totally. It is not like pre internet where you had to change objects, like it is the case in the story of the Ship of Theseus. Digital world knows no inertia.

## Don Toxiqu

The civilization phase transition that we are experiencing is not well distributed even in the USA, which is one of the most advanced nations on this process. Democrats, almost half the population, are still mostly living in the past too. The last presidential race was clear: they were still relying heavily on MSMs and Hollywood stars, but they were unable to propose anything positive that is in harmony with digital times. Yet something makes them unable to update the application. Now the fact that they are losing all across the board can't be masked or ignored anymore. The result is a strange cognitive dissonance that takes the form of Don Quixotism: they are fighting windmills but also normal people, just like Quixote does. Their problem is that they have not adapted to digital culture yet. They are lost on two dimensions: they are the political opposition of the Republicans and they just lost, but they are also lost in their understanding of the world. The fact that they "don't get it" introduces a kind of Larsen effect. They act on the world and don't see the expected results, so they double down. Republicans have understood that. If you think that Musk made a "roman salute", think again. If you think that he is a crypto nazi, think again.

So there are multiple civil wars raging across the west at the same time: both internal to countries but also external to them. The relations between the USA and western Europe is changing fast. It is like the Ship of Theseus is repaired everywhere at the same time, at some point you have to think again what identity means.
