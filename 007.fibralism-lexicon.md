    # The Fibralist Lexicon

    <entry term="Fiber (Fibre)">
      <definition>A long thread of cultural and civilizational continuity
         extended across generations. It represents the temporal dimension of a
         society, acting as a "spacetime object" that prioritizes legacy over 
         territory and material aspects of life in common.</definition>
    </entry>

    <entry term="Fibrizen">
      <definition>The Fibralist version of citizen defined by their active 
         commitment and belonging to a specific Fiber rather than their 
         geographical place of birth. A Fibrizen is a participant in a 
         civilizational project and a cultural legacy.</definition>
    </entry>

    <entry term="Fibralism">
      <definition>A societal ideology designed for the digital era that shifts 
         the focus of communal life from a territorial lens to a temporal and 
         legacy-based lens. It is considered "pre-political" ideology.</definition>
    </entry>

    <entry term="Fibrization">
      <definition>A term replacing "civilization" to center the lexicon on the 
         temporal dimension rather than the "city" (civilis) or location.</definition>
    </entry>

    <entry term="The Four Tiers">
      <definition>A functional taxonomy of human engagement with reality:
        * **Warriors (The Executive)**: Agents who transmute ideas into physical 
          reality through will.
        * **Priests & Judges (The Interpretive)**: Those who assign moral and judicial 
           meaning to objective events.
        * **Merchants & Administration (The Administrative)**: The logistical apparatus 
           ensuring the material continuity of society.
        * **Clerks/Philosophers (The Thinkers)**: The ideological branch operating in 
           the realm of ideas, cultural transmission and evolution.
      </definition>
    </entry>

    <entry term="Antéchrist">
      <definition>Distinct from the "Antichrist" (opponent), the Antéchrist is the one 
         who arrives BEFORE as a herald or sign of the End Times. In this framework, 
         AI and digital transformation act as an Antéchrist to the Enlightenment, 
         they announce the end of the Enlightenment project.
      </definition>
    </entry>

    <entry term="Hyperstition">
      <definition>A fictional or virtual idea that makes itself real through 
         collective belief and the coordination of individual wills.
      </definition>
    </entry>

    <entry term="Ius Civile vs. Ius Gentium">
      <definition>The decoupling of human rights (inherent to existence) from
         civic/Fibric rights (belonging to participants of a specific legacy).
      </definition>
    </entry>

   <entry term="Sesqui-Theonomy">
      <definition>A political and social order organized around a "one-and-a-half" 
         theolonomy. It treats culture as a transcendent wealth that exceeds 
         individual reason, acting as a sacred norm that remains accessible 
         through experience and ritual without requiring prior metaphysical 
         commitment.</definition>
    </entry>

<entry term="Technopraxis">
      <definition>The operational layer of a Fiber, consisting of technical 
         protocols and tools designed to ensure stability and resilience in a 
         high-velocity digital environment. It acts as the 'keel' of the societal 
         ship, providing the necessary ballast to maintain a consistent temporal 
         trajectory and preventing cultural drift without relying on the static 
         or prohibitive nature of traditional written laws.</definition>
    </entry>


   <entry term="Liberty to Bequeath (Bequeathing Liberty)">
      <definition>A third form of liberty, distinct from Ancient (political 
         participation) and Modern (individual privacy) liberties. It is the 
         freedom and duty to act as a temporary steward of a cultural legacy, 
         ensuring that the 'keel' of the Fiber is transmitted to future 
         generations in a resilient and thriving state. It prioritizes long-term 
         continuity over short-term individual discretion.</definition>
    </entry>

  <entry term="The Keel">
      <definition>A metaphor for the dynamic and resilient structural stability 
         of a Fiber. Unlike a 'monument' which is static and vulnerable to the 
         erosion of time, the keel acts as the deep, non-negotiable temporal anchor 
         that provides ballast to the societal ship. It ensures a consistent 
         trajectory through the high-velocity changes of digital culture, allowing 
         for surface-level adaptation while preventing the fundamental dissolution 
         (cultural drift) of the lineage.</definition>
    </entry>
