  <key_concepts>
    <concept name="Two-Way Street">
      Every digital technology is a two-way street. You thought the internet would bring knowledge to you — instead everything about you became public. You thought the smartphone was a magic wand — instead it gradually controls you. You thought social media gave you freedom of expression — instead your expression was turned into a jail (old posts imprison you). You think AI gives you access to wisdom — instead your wisdom is being extracted from you. The one-way promise always inverts.
    </concept>
    <concept name="Miranda Warning Applied to Cookies">
      Cookie consent banners are the modern Miranda warning — a clumsy legal attempt to tell you that the street goes both ways before you enter it. Like Miranda rights in US law, they are technically present but practically ignored. The legal form acknowledges the danger; the user behavior ignores it. An institutional admission of the two-way structure that changes nothing.
    </concept>
    <concept name="Smartphone as Reverse Control">
      The smartphone allows real-time scale-shifting in both directions simultaneously: as an individual you contribute to statistical maps (Google Maps traffic via Android geolocation); as a statistical node you receive targeted individual information. You are simultaneously a data source and a data recipient in real time. "This is truly new. It never existed." Before this, sociologists needed months to collect data; now it is automated and instantaneous.
    </concept>
    <concept name="Freedom of Expression as Prison">
      Social media creates the illusion of free expression while structurally extracting positions. The trend summons you to take a position on a topic you may not care about. Once posted, the post imprisons you — switching opinion proves you are "not serious." Your intellect works to achieve coherence with what the trend forced you to say. The real modern issue is not freedom of speech but freedom of thought.
    </concept>
    <concept name="AI Vampirizes Wisdom">
      The same two-way street applies to LLMs: you think you access the wisdom of the world, but your interactions train the model. Wisdom flows both ways. The hooks at the end of AI responses ("if you need further details...") are the algorithm harvesting more interaction. "Wisdom is extracted from you." Not necessarily bad — but done surreptitiously.
    </concept>
  </key_concepts>

<content>
Smartphones can be seen as a universal remote controllers. They allow you to make food to appear at your home simply by moving fingers. Likewise, a worldwide trip, from the taxi to the airplane flight passing by the booking of hotels, can be ordered with the same device while being half asleep on your couch. The promise of AI agents being that, soon, you won’t even have to care about the details and just have to approve the decisions made by bots working on your behalf. There are all the reasons to belive that this promise will end in the same way that the early promise of the internet —to give global access to knowledge was going to set us free from tyranny— which crashed on the great chinese wall. Because people forget that those technologies are two way streets. You thought that internet was going to bring knowledge to you, instead it is everything about you that has become a public affair. In the same way you thought that the smartphone was a technological version of the magic wand and you end up to be gradually controlled by it... Monster Cookies Cookies are a technological trick to circumvent the limitations of stateless servers. Basically you outsource the state of the conversation that you are having with a website so that the server doesn’t have to bother with maintaining the state (which is complex because of disconnections, security issues, scaling issues etc.). In Sesame street monster cookie eats the cookies. In our real virtual real lives it goes the other way around, you are eaten by the cookies. Indeed there is a rationale behind those irritating banners and click marathons about “cookie policies” that you have to accept without ever reading or thinking about them. The problem comes from the fact that cookies collect what do on website W in order to bring you a smoothest experience when you come back later. At that point there is no problem. But what happens if when you log on site S and S can get your cookie back from your previous interactions that you had with W ? Now replace W and S with mastodon like company Facebook or Google and you start to understand the trap: what you said (or clicked) is going to be used against you to sell you things and ideas tailored to your behavior. So those banners are the modern equivalent of Miranda warning. They are clumsy ways to tell you that Sesame street is a two way street. Remotely controlled Likewise the smartphone sitting in your pocket allows you to gather all the information in the world. But it works the other way around too: the smartphone constantly send information about you to the world (huge corporations). Now the puzzling part is that it allows scale shifting in real time. On one hand, as an individual, you participate in the statistical description of the world. For instance Android is using your geolocation to build traffic maps in real time (the red parts of the roads on Maps). On the other hand you can be targeted as being one representative of a statistical group and sent information tailored for you. The information goes both ways: out of, and towards you at the same time. This is truly new. It never existed. Before you had to wait for sociologists to gather data using biased interviews and produce work. Then, the political would pick and choose, and maybe act along the lines suggested by those works. The process was slow with a multitude of competing actors. Now it is mostly automated and done in real time: Google Maps is going to adapt your travel plans based on those live information (and on with who they are partnering with so that you have lunch at the right spot). Socially controlled On social media you are free to tell what you want. Even if the Twitter files showed that there was a level of shadowbanning and targeted practices, this affair was mostly about direct control of speech. It showed that despite the fact that you were able to publish something, the reach was not a given. Admittedly things have gotten worse on that part, maybe better on the targeted part (but who knows?). But there is a much broader problem consubstantial to the idea of social media that is a two way street issue. Social media are just like smartphones but at a semantic level. If your smartphone gives information about where you go, social media gives data in real time about what people think. And there is no reason to think that it goes in just one direction there too. What it points at is that the real modern issue is not the one about freedom of speech but rather freedom of thought . No one will hear you in your small online media circle if you are not pushed in the back by the Algo. But you will be under the influence of trends. And if you are looking for a modicum of feedback, you will be forced to engage in the “discussion du jour”. On the surface you are giving your opinion, freely, but look at things the other way: your opinion was extracted from you by the trend. Maybe you never wanted to have to take position on that particular discussion. Take the Israel Palestine mess. It is not like factions are fighting over there for centuries, and you are summoned to give your position in a tweet. Now because your intellect will work hard to achieve coherence, you will be prisonner of what you just posted without really thinking about it. Your freedom of expression has been turned into a jail. Because if you switch opinion, your message is still there. It will be used as a proof that you are not serious. Miranda warning again… Vampirized Wisdom At this point of the essay I think you saw it coming: the same two way street story is the crux of the game with AI. You think that you have access to the wisdom of the world through LLMs… But have you noticed how they end up their replies ? With hooks like “If you need further details (e.g., legal analysis, social media trends, or Comey’s motives), let me know!” etc. Actually you are modifying the AI by your interactions, and the AI makes progress with all those feedbacks. Meaning wisdom is extracted from you. I don’t necessarily mean that it is a bad thing. What I am underlying in this essay is that it is done sureptitiously. It is always a good habit to look both ways of the street before crossing, even if it appears to be a one-way street.
