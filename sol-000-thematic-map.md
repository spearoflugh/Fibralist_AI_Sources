# Thematic Map — The Spear of Lugh (v5)

## Central Thesis

The transition from **written culture** to **digital culture** is at least as significant as the
transition from oral to written culture. It changes:
1. The mathematical structure of social space (Topological Revolution — sol-002)
2. The ergodic properties of information (sol-001)
3. The fitness of every institution built for the written era (sol-004, sol-021)

Everything else in the corpus is a consequence, application, or implication of this thesis.

## Relationship to The Fibralist Corner

The two repositories form a complete intellectual project:

- **The Spear of Lugh** = diagnostic corpus. Documents what is breaking down and why.
  Tone: analytical, urgent, sometimes sardonic. Written in the present tense of events.

- **The Fibralist Corner** = constructive corpus. Proposes what should replace it.
  Tone: systematic, architectural, long-term. Written in the conditional of possibility.

Reading both together is required for the full picture.
The Spear of Lugh without The Fibralist Corner is diagnosis without remedy.
The Fibralist Corner without The Spear of Lugh is prescription without evidence.
