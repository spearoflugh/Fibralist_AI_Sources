There are many world famous monuments and places in Venice. Saint Mark’s square is maybe at the top of the list. At one end —the one near the water— there are two columns. One is decorated by a winged lion, the symbol of Venice, and the other one by Saint Theodore slaying a dragon. This area, the Piazzetta San Marco, has a rich history. Surprisingly this history has a lot to do with the unfolding digital revolution. The Winged Lion The winged lion is the symbol of Venice. It sits at the top of one of the columns like an appropriation mark. It is the column on the side close to the Doge’s palace. It is not even clear where those columns came from in the first place. Surely from looting someplace in the eastern part of the Mediterranean area. They are so heavy, and the Venetian ground so shaky, that they stayed down for years until a bright engineer, named Niccolò Barattiero, had the idea to use wet ropes to erect them. When the ropes dry, they shorten their length. Barattiero used this an engine to move the column up. Gradually he achieved a remarkable success and did what no one was able to do for years. Local authorities asked him what he wanted to have as a reward. Niccolò was smarter than most. He didn’t ask for money or any material good. He simply requested the right to organize gambling between the two columns. Gambling was forbidden at the time in Venice. The Doge was not a fool either, and while more or less forced to accept, he decided to curse the place by performing public executions at the exact same place. The idea was to frighten Venetians from gambling in a place so haunted with ghosts. Reading this story with 2025 eyes is just crazy. The fact that DOGE was a thing at the start of the year is the least surprising parallel echo from the past. If one wanted to prove that “history repeats itself” is a law of nature, I bet that the story of the bright engineer fighting with wit the political power would come as a central piece. No doubt that the fact that the administration was clever would not help the case though… Of course the most striking element echoing with our era is that today online betting is all the rage. It is, in some typical sense, the same story —and a different story— because online betting can be done everywhere from your smartphone. Before the digital revolution you had to go places to bet: casinos and specific geographic areas. It is a bit like porn before the internet. You had to physically go to a theater and at least overcome a bit of shame to let yourself slide into vice. Digital empowerment is isotropic. It works the same in every direction, good or bad. And of course the bad direction is always the easiest one. The wings of the winged lion are the symbols of spiritual elevation, divine inspiration, and resurrection . They link the creature to the divine, symbolizing the Evangelist’s spiritual mission and Venice’s belief in its divine protection. We used to build such monuments as a way to educate people—before the internet, and even before literacy was a common thing—as a warning also. Today’s tourists will at best make a selfie in front of the column. Commodification as lifestyle. Using Polymarket it is now possible to gamble on *everything*. When I say everything, it is really everything that you can imagine. At the time of writing, one of the top gamble market is: The CEO of Kalshi , another online platform for performing “prediction market” trades, statement is as clear as you can imagine, the goal is: “financialize everything and create a tradable asset out of any difference in opinion.” Kalshi CEO Says He Wants to Monetize ‘Any Difference in Opinion’ , Gizmodo, December 4, 2025 . Let me spare you some time: it has barely anything to do with capitalism, finance or cryptocurrencies. It is the same old story of gambling but 1000x. Aren’t digital technologies wonderful? For once those are purely zero-sum games. The losers are paying the winners. One way to see it would be to say that it is a tax on stupidity. But for that to be credible, it would require you to believe that it is a story of skill. While this story has some merits on markets, because betting on the right industry indeed helps growth, here nothing is funded. Plus there are limitless ways to cheat, meaning to consciously build insider/outsider divide. It works at every level. There is this unfolding scandal in the Turkish football league , leading (until now) to the suspension of roughly a thousand players … Beware of Insider knowledge I bet that you were not thinking about that when they announced the era of information superhighways in the mid-1990s. You were not thinking that the global village would turn into a universal casino filled with OnlyFans “models”. The idea was rather that by giving access to the whole of humanity’s knowledge and wisdom, and by providing better communication, it would lead to the empowerment of the citizen. A new Renaissance… The future looked bright. The slayed Dragon Slaying dragons used to be a thing under the Byzantine Empire. There are many such stories, Theodore is one of the oldest —maybe Saint George, another cross reference to our era with what is goingon in the UK , is more famous— and was the patron of Venice before Saint Mark became the new boss in 828. This explains why Theodore is on the top of the second column of the Piazzetta San Marco. I find it easier to spot the dragon than its victor today. But if we believe the myth, things went like this: it is said that a formidable dragon terrorized the population that lived near Theodore’s encampment. The saint set out in search of the monster, found it, and killed it with a spear thrust to the head. He understood this victory as a sign that God judged him ready to face martyrdom. He ended up burned alive after having been tortured because he refused to bow down and honor pagan gods and set fire to a temple of the “Mother of the Gods” (Cybele). Free speech and consequences, you know. I am failing to recognize any sign of a Theodore today. The dragon is ravaging the internet with a strength and precision that couldn’t have been foreseen by Middle Ages theologians. Every dopamine hack has been exploited by new information technologies to a degree of sophistication to the like of a rocket going to the moon. Every bad human impulse has been weaponized. It is not only the Fentanyl in the streets of Philadelphia —meaning bortherly love in greek, did you know?— or the exploding cocaine boats off the coast of Venezuela , but the gambling that is hidden in every pocket today. Maybe gambling is the worse sin because it is something like waiting for the grace for nothing. At least people engaging in prostitution or drug consumption do something to deserve their personal hell. In gambling nothing is done, you wait for the good fortune to chose you. Because. Drug and prostitution are an assault on morality but gambling is the pure negation of the idea of merit itself. It aims at the destruction of the source of good in a chemically pure way. Gambling is the ultimate dragon and now thanks to technology it is a distributed dragon. This suggest than nothing short of a distributed Theodore can fight it successfully.
