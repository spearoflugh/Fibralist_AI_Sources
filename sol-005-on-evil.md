Following the advice of Beyon Miloyan on Twitter, I have just read " La Part du Diable ". This essay by Denis de Rougemont has been published in 1942. It made a lasting impression on me. I was not the only one because after reading this book H. Arendt's reaction was the following: "le problème du mal sera la question fondamentale de l’après-guerre pour la vie intellectuelle en Europe – comme la mort est devenue le problème fondamentalaprès la dernière guerre" “The problem of evil will be the fundamental question of the post-war intellectual life in Europe – just as death became the fundamental problem after the last war." If this essay struck me, it is because it is directly tackling with many issues that I am trying to adress in this substack for years now. The fact that it was written so long ago, before even the idea of computers and of networks was alien to every human, only makes it more interesting. In this book the author think at the issue of Evil in our modern world. He clearly foresees the trap in which our society has completely fallen into. The externalisation of the Evil - in the figure of Hitler - have hijacked our duty to work on our inner evils. By turning an internal affair into a public one, we have jumped into the first and most ancient trap of the Devil: to make you believe that it doesn't exist in the first place. This process of oblivion is so well described in the essay - and the implications for our society are so well foreseen - that I am not going to talk about that here. Find a translation or read it (it is in free accesss on theuniversity of Geneva Website in original french version): much better than anythingI could come up with. But this essay has motivated into thinking about what can be said today with relation to the foreseable future. Here I am going to talk about what shape the Evil could take in a digital society. From Scarcity to Abundance The shift towards a digital society can also be seen in the light of a post industrial era transition. The problem of the industrial era was in the production side. The question was to be able to produce a ton of goods in the cheapest way possible. The "american dream" of the 1950s was the mass equalization of material conditions across the globe. Eventually everyone got a house in a nice suburban area, with cars, refrigerators, cable tv, computers, internet access, smartphones, frequent airflights across the world ... you name it. Now that everyone has an Iphone (on a global scale), we have moved into a new kind of society. The problem is no longer to tackle with scarcity but with abundance. We have too much food (think obesity, diabetes etc.), too much information (mis/mal/disinformation and the equivalent of fast food with preprocessed news), too much spare time (with an ever shrinking attention span going on with it) etc. We have too much of what we had to few and it creates new issues. Those issuesare like the dual twins of our old problems. It is very disturbing because for most of human history we had to deal with scarcity everywhere. Even the extension of the life expectancy can be analyzed through this lens. Maybe it makes it more difficult to have functionning multi decades mariages. Having to live in couples for more than half a century was never a reality (or something very rare) for most of humans... You can multiply the examples at will but I think that it is clear that abundance comes with its own problems. We are not accustomed to them. As problems are changing, the way to tackle with them will also evolve. And so will do Evil. The important point being that Evil will take unusual/novel forms. The first step is to identify and being aware of them. Control Freaks The Evil comes with freedom. What directly follows from this observation is that humans try to control the Evil by controlling freedom. It is done both internally (morality) and externally (laws). The priest and the warrior are the archetypal expressions of that. They are dual versions of one another. You can say that the priest is controlling the material world via spiritual means while the warrior works the other way around. The warrior is using material means (force) to enforce spiritual things -e.g. "this piece of land is mine, not yours". This is why, inevitably, the problem of Evil manifests itself through control. In the industrial revolution era the problem was to make things. Hence the political fight was about the control of means of production. The fight between Marxism and classical liberalism is precisely centered around that issue. The cold war between the USA and the USSR being the epitome of it. Thinking that the problem was solved circa 1992 when the USSR dissolved, and the cold war ended, is the usual trick of the Devil: to make you believe that it doesn't exist. If it is clear on the geostrategic front (just look at what is unfolding in Ukraine). It is also clear on broader scale. Now the control problem is all about the *consumption/use of goods and ideas*. Let's examine some recent examples illustrating this idea. Degrowth crowd . This is in some sense the most fundamental and extremist version. The idea would be that we are too many and are exhausting the planet. Blah blah (you know the song). The idea is plain and simple: to stop producing goods and even humans. Not allowing the use of what works . The example of Germany shutting down perfectly ok nuclear plants that emits no carbon emission, in the name of the ecological transition makes no sense from any point of view but from this flip on control that we are witnessing. Institutional war on ideas . The fact that institutions redefine words on a daily basis is also new. When a supreme court nominee cannot even reply to the question "what is a woman ?" in a public hearing tells you more than you need to know. This is the 1984 idea of controling thinking by controling language. There are too many information sources available. The administration can no longer control the production of ideas so it tries to control the use of ideas through language manipulation. Vaccine passports . The COVID crisis revealed how much our societies have changed. The use of smartphones to implement a kind of authorization based citizenship is also an example that speaks for itself. ESG stamp of approval . The ESG scores are also a pure manifestation of this new kind of control. It is done through "governance" means. It is a mix of legislation, financial and insurance (you need to reach x score in order to have an insurance etc.) means. Here again we see that there is not an attempt to directly control the means of production. Just how they are going to be used (no diesel car in my 15 minutes city) via more and more tightened specification of what has to be done in order to be legal (by piling up new legislations). Abundance Authoritarianism and Inclusion The fact that our political institutions are evolving is not necessarily evil. What I have just described is also a way to instantiate the political power in a digital society. But as Plato noticed in the Republic, every form of government comes with its degenerate version. The question is: what will be the degenerate version of control in a digital society ? Another way to put it: what will be the authoritarian forms in an in a society of abundance ? The previous forms of authoritarianism were based on exclusion. Nazism, Communism and co all were versions of exclusion authoritarianism. What does it mean in practice: when a group or an individual is identified as a foe, they are excluded from society. A bullet in the neck being the most draconian version (you can never go back). The process of exclusion is unique and definitive. Youare expelled like an alien via the immune system of society. Somehow it rhymes with the production part: this its dark counterpart. I think that future forms of authoritarianisms will be based on inclusion . The idea here is that you will have to continually prove that you are a good member of the society by abiding by new rules and regulations. Basically every instant of your life will be managed as if life would occurr in a computer operating system. This is the idea of Vaccine passports. Using smartphones, but it can by your iris - Worldcoin - or your hand - like in those cheap old dystopian novels - you will have to display the right QR-code to be part of the society. Unlike the exclusion version it is a continual process that never ends. The fact that the meaning of words change is not without reminding the ever changing TOS of social media for instance. Don't expect old style fascist mobs either. The people who are goint to implement this rampant authoritarianism are no longer thugs wandering in the streets with big clubs in hand. They will be people whose job is to control the flow of information. Because their club will be to shut your access to the network: something like an institutionnalized version of “cancel culture”. In the previous instantiations, the administration (seen as the control aparatus of the state) was at the service of the state. You used administrative staff to track people that you were sending to concentration camps. In the new version they will be the concentration camps themselves: you are just denying the access to normal life to your targets. This is possible because of the new information technologies. By using networks you can centralize and personalize authorisation live. Think about how many times you identify yourself to access a service. Notice how different it is from previous society in which if you paid with cash you could access services. Passports (and more generally institutionalized identity proofs) were not a thing before the end of World War I. If you want to have a glimpse of the future just look at how the Truck for freedom movemement in Canada has been delt with. You know it: they froze the bank accounts of demonstrators … and people who supported the demonstration via donations: "The scope of these measures will be time-limited, geographically targeted, as well as reasonable and proportionate to the threats they are meant to address," Trudeau said, adding that he doesn't plan to deploy the military. You see ? There is no longer the need to have thugs on the street when you can shut down the means of living from a safe distance. Of course everything that is said here has to be understood in an upside down manner. It is not going to be time-limited, nor geographically targeted. Expect unreasonable and not proportionate use of this mean of action.
