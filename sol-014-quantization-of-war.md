Until quantum physics emerged from the first couple of decades of the XX° everything looked fine. Newton and Leibniz gave us very smooth mathematical theories describing how the world evolves: from classical mechanics to the law of continuity . Even in war their overall philosophy applied very well: WW1, WW2, Korean War, Gulf Wars 1 and 2 were all wars fought following the idea of a continuous deformation of front lines . The Graal, from a military point of view, was to be able to puncture the line and exploit the breach. But the bread and butter of industrial war was the impact of superior industrial output. Basically : if you were able to outproduce your enemy, you were going to win. Tactical ingenuity and even technological lead could help or harm (depending on which sides those factors were displayed) the general tendency of the war, but at the end of the day if the USA can produce 96,000 aircrafts vs 40,000 for Germany and 28,000 for Japan … well everyone knows how it ends. So from a topological point of view the industrial capacity could be seen as a force continuously transforming the front line until the enemy could no longer resist. We are now entering in a digital society. It has deep implications in all areas of life including in the way wars are fought and should be thought. Planck’s Constant War is this activity that many people do wrong while being certain of doing it right. The most common trap is to be ready to fight for the previous war. From the ligne Maginot to the elusive search of the decisive battle by the Japanese during WW2 —trying to replay the 1905 battle of Tsushima that eliminated Russia's navy from the Pacific area— war history books are full of examples of strategists who had understood everything… about past confrontations. But war is not a TV quizz game. Si vis pacem, para bellum is not enough. You have to prepare for the right war because war is not a constant through history. What is invariably true is that if you don’t adapt you are going to lose. The natural question becomes : what are the features of post industrial warfare ? Wave Corpuscle Duality Imagine that you go back in time and bring IT capabilities —observation and communication in real time— to Hannibal, Caesar or Napoleon, and nothing else (no new weapons). How do you think that would have changed their ways to wage war? You can push the thought experiment a bit further : just add precision to the weapon systems in charge, again do not change any other characteristics of the weapon systems. What happens? Those thought experiments are relevant because new information technologies specifically change weapon systems over those dimensions: knowledge, communication and precision. The first thing that is noticeable is that precision gains in importance as time goes on : it would have helped Caesar’s legion but not that much. Most of the fight was done with spears and swords. Now when you add a JDAM kit to a 500 pounds gravity bomb it is another story. Remember that during WW2 bombing was mostly, mostly as in 80% of the bombs missed their marks , wasted. It means that with only acting on this dimension alone you instantly gain 5x by adding precision. The second points that emerges from this exercise is the positive reinforcement loop between those three characteristics. With better knowledge of the battlefield paired with the ability to communicate this knowledge in real time and high precision ammunition you are building a system that transforms the same WW2 bomb into a deadly system even with 2025 standards. Of course there were improvements in explosives, armors, engines etc. but those improvements are largely marginal. The cocktail of knowledge, communication and precision, on its own, is a real revolution. It changes everything, by that I understand something to the importance of the introduction of artillery: armies (the way they are structured) our ways to understand warfare (tactics logistics etc.) are radically different. On top of that there is another revolution : more and more technologies are dual purpose. Rather, it has become easy, usual and beneficial to weaponize civil technologies. The first thing that comes to mind are off the shelf drones: just equip them with a handgrenade and you have a FPV drone. Reminder : 70% of deaths on Ukraine battlefield are by those dual purpose technos. Likewise the use of Starlink, initially a civil techonology, has proven instrumental in Ukraine and even Iran theaters. Schrödinger Peace Have you noticed that the number of formal war declarations, following tradition or international laws, has almost vanished? Neither what happens in Ukraine, Gaza or Iran are officially wars. People use to joke about the Putin choice of words —special operation— but nowadays only third world countries like Sudan (the last formally declared war dates back…2012) actually do the job of formally declaring wars. Yet the number of people dying in armed conflicts is already today around 600 people a day. We are not in the waters of world wars statistics, but it is already something comparable to the thirty years war (in raw numbers, admittedly the population was nowhere near what it is today). Thus, there is no clear distinction between war and peace. For instance the “Vietnam war” was not a formal war . But the number of armed conflicts is rising and the intensity of fights (who remembers what happened between the Houthis and the USA just few weeks ago?) gives this strange feeling of a schrödinger peace: while never in war, the world is never at peace either. Like the famous cat, that is alive and dead at the same time, both states that appear exclusive are in fact superposed. Double-slit Mediasphere The increase of knowledge is also working at a meta-level. Armies know that they are seen in real time, and it has a sizeable influence in their way to conduct the war. Just like in the double-slit experiment , the fact of being observed actually changes the material world. Modern conflicts all have a media dimension because every private is now equipped with a smartphone and can, sometimes live, broadcast his experience. It means that every conflict has also a social media front (the ghost of Kiev etc.). Also some military actions are done specifically for PR. One may think at the Ukrainian spider’s web attack of June the first . From a purely militaristic point of view the attack has close to 0 effect on the material battelfied, but it is huge on the virtual battelfield. Maybe the idea was to have extra bargainning chips? It is anyone’s guess at this point but one thing is clear : not only such an operation would be impossible without TIC, but this operation wouldn’t have been scheduled at all if there were no media to relay it. In that also the new information technologies change the way warfare is evolving. Our culture has changed its state very abruptly. We jumped from a written culture to a digital culture in less than an decade. Another quantum leap.
