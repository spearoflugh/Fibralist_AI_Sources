The story of Brunelleschi ’s discovery of vanishing point is not well known, yet it could be argued it marked the starting point of the Renaissance. Brunelleschi showed that you can map a 3D scene on a 2D plane and proved his point both geometrically and experimentally: He painted an exact image of a Baptistery on a wooden panel using his new mathematical painting method. He drilled a small peephole in the center of the painting. He stood in front of the real building, looked through the hole from the back, and held a mirror in front of the painting. By moving the mirror in and out of view, he could compare the painted reflection directly against the real building: perfect matching! The guy was following the science ™️ before the concept was invented few centuries later by Francis Bacon . It is a remarkable feat of the human mind and shall remind the reader that not everything is about physics or dry mathematical results. Art is an integral part of the human journey. The vanishing point is an extraordinary invention that changed everything, but that paradoxically remained unseen. Because there is no actual vanishing point on the painting. Yet everything revolves around it. Now think at AIs from this perspective (pun intended). The promise of AI is the one of a digital vanishing point: you will never see it directly yet every part of your life is going to be impacted by it. Front View The most direct use of a vanishing point in art is the front view. This was Brunelleschi’s original technique. It amounts to the first, most intuitive, use of AI: You submit a prompt, that is essentially a query, in order to have an answer. The AI is used as a super search engine that builds answers tailored to the user’s interest. The AI saves the effort of having to go through documents found in the web search. Like in the front view, it is a view that flattens the world. A single interface to know all that there is to know. Like the One Ring , the AI is this unique point towards which all knowledge converge. Corner View Perspective in art can be more complex; the corner view is also known as the two-points perspective. This is used when you are looking at the corner of an object. You see two sides of the object receding in different directions. Vertical lines remain straight up and down, but all horizontal lines angle toward one of the two vanishing points. In AI terms it amounts to the use of the AI for comparing different bodies of work, and for writing executive summaries out of a set of references. With all the known, or not, limitations of LLMs this is a work of perspective the AI is asked to perform, in the sense that it has to compose from multiple sources and blend them in a meaningful way. Extreme View The most advanced way to use vanishing points to give a perspective in a painting is the three-points perspective. It came later because this is used when looking up at a very tall object or down from a great height (something like the bird’s eye view of a city). Three vanishing points are used: two on the horizon, and one high above or far below. Even the vertical lines (like the sides of a skyscraper) converge. If looking up, they taper toward a point in the sky (or on the ground if looking down). This is the generative use of AIs. The AIs are no longer used solely to access already existing information but as a partner to create new things. The role of the horizontal lines There is hidden element within the hidden element of perspective work. The vanishing point is hidden but where does this non-existent point sit in the first place? This is the role of the horizontal line in art. Basically it can be seen as the height of the viewer’s eye. From the AI point of view, it is the role of the profile. When you access an AI, you need to register. Even if you are using a free version —beware of the well known adage: “If you’re not paying for it, you’re not the customer; you’re the product being sold”— a profile of you is being made. This isn’t inherently bad, but it means that, as time goes by, the AI’s answer are going to be tailored to you. It can be for the best, for instance given your questions, over time, the AI may adjust to your level of knowledge and build more interesting a swears for the same question. Maybe you are enjoying technical details or conversely you prefer a 30,000-foot view, and maybe it depends on the subject. This is unknown to the user who is more and more only interacting with his favorite AI model. This is the hidden part of the hidden part. Relativity Freud was not just a sexually obsessed doctor interested in knowing what your secret thoughts about your close family are, and while it is true that psychoanalysis does not meet the standard of what we would call science, with all the quotation marks that you can imagine around the word, he was nonetheless a very bright thinker. His theory of the three narcissistic blows is worthy of few thoughts. The three blows are all related to the relativity of the human experience. A new perspective one would say. The first one is cosmological — Copernicus shows that the Sun doesn’t revolve around the Earth; it’s the other way around— the second one is biological —Darwin argued with his theory of evolution that humans are (descendants of) animals and not a unique creation— and the third one psychological —Freud expressed the idea that you, meaning the conscious part of your mind, are not even the master of your own ship due to the unconscious—. With AIs comes a fourth blow. Maybe intelligence is no longer a unique human trait and mere machines can think as well. We are not there yet, and in my opinion there are many deep theoretical objections to that possibility, but this is not the place for such a discussion. The question, however, is not without merit. Even if current AIs are demonstrably stupid, it is impossible to deny that huge progress has been made during those last three years (yesterday, November 30th marked the 3rd birthday of chatGPT’s public release). No one really knows where, and if, the progress will stop. A digital Renaissance The Renaissance was not a single thing. It was not just the invention of the vanishing point that defined the Renaissance. It is a much larger phenomenon in which factors interplayed with one another: arts, architecture, science, philosophical renewal etc. It has also been sparked by not so positive events like the Black Death or the fall of Constantinople. Such events shook the society to its core and were an integral part of this complex era of transformation. AI is just one factor. It is a big one, but ultimately it is just one component. Other phenomenons are happening at the same time, and we are just at the onset : social media’s disruption of the political campaigns, the failings of several institutions, the end of the post World-War II international order etc. Exciting times even if there is a storm coming .
