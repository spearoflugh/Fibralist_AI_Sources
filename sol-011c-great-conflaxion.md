  <key_concepts>
    <concept name="Conflaxion — Forced Conflation of Real and Virtual Worlds">
      The "Great Conflaxion" is the forced convergence of the virtual world (ideas, no space
      or time, no degraded mode) onto physical reality (objects, constraints, resilience
      through friction). The everything app (Twitter/X under Musk) and Worldcoin (iris-scan
      biometric identity wallet) are the twin instantiations: they operate on the philosophy
      that the virtual point of view should be imposed on reality — "a toddler trying to fit
      a disc into a square hole." The tale of Procrustes 3.0: instead of cutting the guest
      to fit the bed, the world is cut to fit the digital architecture. Hubris in the Greek
      sense: negation of the material world's bearing on life. The loop between ideas and
      reality (Jacob's ladder: angels moving up and down) is destroyed when the two are
      conflated. "By conflating the real world with the virtual world this loop goes away."
    </concept>
    <concept name="Inclusion Authoritarianism — New Flavor of Control">
      Classical authoritarianism operated through exclusion: identify the enemy → exclude →
      death as terminal exclusion. Digital authoritarianism operates through inclusion:
      you are always included but under ever-changing rules that you must follow or your
      life becomes incrementally more complex. "You will be able to use the everything
      network if you apply by the rules of the day that are continually changing."
      Worldcoin: one iris = one identity = one financial life = complete controllability.
      The COVID vaccine pass is the prototype: not exclusion from society (you can still
      breathe) but inclusion-under-condition that can be revoked at any point. This inverts
      the natural rights framework: instead of rights flowing from existence (you are →
      you have rights), rights become grants that can be rescinded by rule-change.
      The ABC paradigm (sol-000j) from the institutional side; the Conflaxion is the
      technological infrastructure that makes it possible.
    </concept>
    <concept name="Digital Technologies Have No Degraded Mode">
      Cash is resilient: everyone can verify a banknote and use it without infrastructure.
      A fully digital system is binary — works perfectly or fails completely. The
      everything app / Worldcoin architecture concentrates all functions in a single stack
      (from space satellites to glass manufacturing to quantum mechanics for WiFi to
      GPS relativity corrections). Each link is a systempunkt (sol-000b). A Carrington
      event or EMP attack on a fully integrated digital society = total collapse with no
      degraded mode. "The bigger the honeypot the smarter the hackers." Concentration of
      identity + finance in one system creates the largest attack surface in history —
      the only certainty is that it will be hacked.
    </concept>
    <concept name="Unity Before/After — The Real Singularity">
      The inflection point at which the search for unity shifts from good (gathering people
      around an inclusive platform before an election) to evil (using state apparatus to
      force unity after an election — legitimate violence). "This is the same difference
      as there is between listening and talking/giving orders." The technological version:
      removing humans from automated systems to force their functioning. The Chinese
      automated ports example. Hubris as the original sin: acting as if the material
      world has no bearing. "A city is an equilibrium between will and forces of nature."
      The Conflaxion eliminates the wrestling with reality — the heroes' dance on the
      edge of what is possible — and replaces it with a system that has removed the edge.
    </concept>

<content>
Just a few days out of the matrix can bring you a radical shift in perspective. All it took was a chess tournament and a small journey in Italy for the blue bird to be turned into an art déco X. The everything app is at the confluence of many historical trends. Digital technologies have blurred the line between ideas and objects. This is a complex philosophical issue that has puzzled humanity for millennia. Actually it is a complex web of many interrelated issues with retroaction loops everywhere. The concomitant launch of Worldcoin is an echo of the same tectonic shift that we are experiencing.

## Every every

Everywhere, everything, everyone and everytime. The virtual world is not like meat space. In the virtual world there is not really a notion of time nor space. You can dig up a tweet that is a decade old as easily as you can send a new tweet. The fact that the message was posted from Kazakhstan or Tahiti is also not relevant to the difficulty of retrieving the message. You can't hear what someone is telling just a few meters away when you are not there when it is said. Those mere material limits do not exist in the virtual world. If you don't like the people in your gym you can go to another gym. There is no other internet. This is why pseudonyms can be useful in the virtual world: they allow you to build an equivalent of those material limits. Under a pseudonym you can build a reputation (the track record of your exchanges): honest players can be sorted out of trolls fairly easily and at the same time you can change pseudonym if you want. You will have to work again to build a new reputation but everything comes with a cost.

The everything app and Worldcoin operate on a totally different philosophy. They totally embrace the virtual point of view and actively try to force it on reality. Picture a toddler trying to fit a disc into a square hole. But those kids have *a lot* of power. They can push really hard. What will happen is the tale of Procrustes 3.0. Let's examine a few issues to have a glimpse of what will — not could — go wrong. Notice that all those problems have been addressed in this substack in previous publications. The fact that those projects are the epitome of our problems to enter a digital society is not surprising. In no particular order:

**Identity.** Conflating identity with a number (or a name which is the same thing) is very primitive and technologically shaky. Philosophers have pondered on the subject of identity for millennia for a reason. Moreover in cyber security it is known that even if identity is by definition "the thing that does not change," *proof of identity* should always change. Otherwise replay attacks are too easy to implement.

**Identity theft.** If your identity is, one way or another, conflated with your iris then stealing your identity or impersonating you towards this network is going to be easy. Cameras are more and more precise, as well as various 3D printers. At the end of the day your eyeball is just the support to a number: whatever is the output of the machine observing your eyeball, you can physically read that number and use it later.

**Inheritance.** What happens to your wallet when you die? Is it blocked because your eyeball is no longer here? If not it means there are "other means" to perform a transaction… Whatever those other means may be, it means that the system can be hacked.

**It works until it doesn't work at all.** Digital technologies have no degraded mode. Just like 0/1. It works perfectly or not at all. This is one idea behind the movie Battleship in which an old battleship is used to destroy top-notch alien ships. Current system still has cash: it is very resilient. Everyone can check a banknote/coin and use it.

**The bigger the honeypot the smarter the hackers.** The incentives to hack the system will be larger than ever. If you concentrate everything in a single app/wallet it will act as a gigantic magnet for hackers. And the system will be hacked — there will be scams etc. It is the only thing that I am 100% certain about.

**On the edge of the world.** Those propositions are not only smartphone applications. They work because they sit at the end of a technology stack that is impressive: from space to glass and mining industry, from quantum mechanics (for WiFi) to relativity (for geopositioning). It appears to be resilient but who knows what a severe Carrington event, or an EMP attack, would cause in a fully integrated digital society.

## Unity — before and after

There is an inflection point when the search for unity switches from being good to becoming evil. This is the real singularity. In politics it happens around elections. Searching for unity before the election is good — you gather people around to build an inclusive platform. Once in charge everything changes because you can force unity using the state apparatus (legitimate violence). It can become authoritarian and dictatorial before you know it. This is the same difference as there is between listening and talking/giving orders.

Those tectonic technological shifts to conflate the real and the virtual worlds are fraught with similar dangers. The first thing you do when you want to use robots at large scale in the real world is to remove humans — consider the gigantic automated ports of China. Hubris was the main sin in ancient times, before Christianity. Hubris is the negation of the fact that there is a material world that you have to take into account. You cannot act as if the material aspects of life have no bearings on your life. On the other hand humans are not subjected to nature like other animals. A city is just that: an equilibrium between will and forces of nature. If both material and virtual worlds are artificially conflated, troubles appear. The Greek Mythology can be considered from this sole perspective. The respect to Gods is the idea of taking into accounts the natural forces. Heroes will dance on the edge of what is possible. Heroes defy Gods to steal the good from them. The fact that "Israel" literally means "wrestled with God" can come to mind too.

By conflating virtual and real world — or more precisely by making it as if the real world is no longer relevant — you skip the wrestling part with reality. We all know how it ends.

## Inclusion forever

Another distinctive feature of this conflation is that it changes the nature of authoritarianism. We all know how past authoritarian institutions worked. They mainly worked with exclusion. If you were identified as an enemy the implication was that you were excluded from society. Death being the most irreversible version of this exclusion procedure. Once excluded from the good society it was quick and with no return.

I think that the authoritarianism that will come in a digital society will come with a different flavor. It will be more something like an inclusion thing. It may look counter-intuitive because we discuss more of the accounts that have been suspended (very exclusion style) than the general case. The inclusion authoritarian acts continually by providing new rules that have to be followed otherwise your life is just made more complex. Everyday comes with a new rule to abide by and you are never fully included. It is a never-ending process. This is the same idea that we have seen at play during the COVID crisis when vaccine passports were required to have a normal life. Rules were changing by the day. This is the polar opposite of the "natural rights" idea that, just because *you are* you have rights. You will be able to use the everything network if you apply by the rules of the day that are continually changing. Having one identity — one number — that allows you to have a financial life gives you no choice. What will happen when the powers that be decide that you can't do X with your Worldcoin? It is much easier to control than cash or gold. You will be included by force in *the system* and you will have to abide by the new rules if you want to continue to be part of society.

Once again I am back to Jacob's ladder. On the ladder there are angels continually moving up and down between the earth and the sky. My interpretation is that life is a continuous loop between ideas and reality. By conflating the real world with the virtual world this loop goes away. Saying that I am having very bad vibes about all that is the understatement of the century. Most of what I am feeling about that is below the level of being verbalized. I feel that this is *the* real subject, the dual of the sempiternal trending thing on social media.
